Ray Zirui Xu

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Aerospace Engineering at University of Michigan, advised by Vasileios Tzoumas. My research interests lie in the intersection of optimization, control, and robotics. I aim to enable large-scale distributed collaborative autonomy in complex tasks, for vehicles, drones, and mobile sensors. In particular, my work investigates:

  • Theories: Provably near-optimal algorithms for combinatorial optimization and online combinatorial learning;
  • Applications: Robot learning and coordination algorithms that enable effective and efficient online decision-making in dynamic and adversarial environments;
  • Applications: Real-world implementation of coordination algorithms onboard resource-constrained agents for communication- and computation-demanding tasks.

I'm currently a Research Intern at Honda Research Institute for summer 2024.

Previously, I received an M.S. in Electrical & Computer Engineering from Georgia Tech in 2020 and a B.Eng. in Automation from Northeastern University (China) in 2018.
